Where the Money Goes

The $10,000 Threshold

Under College Park procurement rules, any expenditure of $10,000 or more triggers City Council review. Of 151 invoices paid to RSW Services, only one ever crossed that line. The rest — $680,059 worth — were structured below that line.

The Statistical Anomaly

If invoice amounts were naturally distributed, you would not expect the $9,000–$9,999 range (a single $1,000 band) to contain more total dollars than any other $2,000 range. Yet it does: $265,877 sits in that narrow band — more than the $0–$999, $1K–$2,999, or $7K–$8,999 ranges.

Data Source & Methodology

All data comes from official City of College Park Accounts Payable Invoice Reports for Vendor 4409 (RSW Services, Inc.), generated by the City's financial system on 02/19/2026. Three reports were analyzed: FY24 (Jul 2023–Jun 2024), FY25 (Jul 2024–Jun 2025), and partial FY26 (Jul 2025–Jan 2026).

The $10,000 threshold is based on College Park's purchasing policy, which requires City Council review for individual expenditures at or above that amount. RSW Services, Inc. was approved as a vendor for "Minor Repairs — Under $10,000" per City records.

"Back-to-back" clusters include only invoices with the same or very similar work descriptions filed within 7 days. Unrelated work performed on the same day is not flagged as splitting.
