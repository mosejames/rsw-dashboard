The $10,000 Threshold

Under College Park procurement rules, any expenditure of $10,000 or more requires City Council approval. Of 151 invoices paid to RSW Services, only one ever crossed that line. The rest — $680,059 worth — were approved without a council vote.

The Statistical Anomaly

If invoice amounts were naturally distributed, you would not expect the $9,000–$9,999 range (a single $1,000 band) to contain more total dollars than any other $2,000 range. Yet it does: $265,877 sits in that narrow band — more than the $0–$999, $1K–$2,999, or $7K–$8,999 ranges.

Data Source & Methodology

All data comes from official City of College Park Accounts Payable Invoice Reports for Vendor 4409 (RSW Services, Inc.), generated by the City's financial system on 02/19/2026. Three reports were analyzed: FY24 (Jul 2023–Jun 2024), FY25 (Jul 2024–Jun 2025), and partial FY26 (Jul 2025–Jan 2026).

The $10,000 threshold is based on College Park's purchasing policy, which requires City Council approval for individual expenditures at or above that amount. Invoice splitting refers to the practice of dividing what could be a single purchase into multiple smaller invoices to avoid this threshold.

RSW Services, Inc. was approved as a vendor for "Minor Repairs — Under $10,000" per City records.
